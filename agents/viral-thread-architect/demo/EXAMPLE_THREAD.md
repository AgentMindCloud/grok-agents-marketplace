### 1/10

Most "multi-agent" systems are one model wearing three wigs. If the agents don't have different instructions, tools, and stop conditions, you don't have a swarm. You have a loop.

> **[IMAGE]** `hero.png`
> **Alt text:** Three thin cyan-bordered panels labeled coordinator, creative, logic, floating over a black background with faint circuit traces pulsing between them.

### 2/10

Start by writing the orchestration contract before you touch the agents. Who calls whom. What each one returns. Where the handoff fails. If the contract fits on one screen, you can ship it.

### 3/10

The single biggest cost driver in a swarm is the coordinator waiting on a slow agent. Set a per-agent timeout. Set a global `max_parallel_calls`. The first time you hit the ceiling, you'll find a runaway prompt.

### 4/10

Personas are not flavor text. They are a compression format. "Lucas writes hooks. No cliches. No emojis." replaces three paragraphs of instruction the model would otherwise ignore.

### 5/10

Encrypt the swarm's working state. Set persistence to `session-only`. Long-lived multi-agent memory is where PII leaks get born.

> **[IMAGE]** `state.png`
> **Alt text:** Diagram of a session-scoped encrypted state ring connecting three agent nodes, cyan border, black background.

### 6/10

Log every tool call. Log every prompt. Log every retry. A swarm you can't replay is a swarm you can't debug, and a swarm you can't debug will ship a bad post.

### 7/10

Put a hard cost ceiling on the agent, not just the API key. Per-day and per-invocation. The invocation ceiling is what catches the prompt-injection "write forever" attack before your bill does.

### 8/10

Every external write needs a human approval gate. Not once a week. Every write. The moment you automate approval, the swarm starts shipping the mean of its training data.

### 9/10

If your swarm can't explain why it delegated to the agent it picked, you don't have delegation. You have a gacha. Surface the routing reason in the trace. Read it every day for the first month.

> **[IMAGE]** `trace.png`
> **Alt text:** A compact three-row trace view showing coordinator routing decisions with cyan highlight on the delegation step.

### 10/10

Multi-agent isn't magic. It's a contract between models with different jobs, enforced by timeouts, budgets, and an audit log. Build it that way and the swarm works. Skip any of those and you're back to one model in three wigs.
